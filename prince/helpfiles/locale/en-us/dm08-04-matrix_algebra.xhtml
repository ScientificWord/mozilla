Systems of Linear Equations You identify a system of equations by entering the equations in an n × 1 matrix, with one equation to a row. When you have the same number of unknowns as equations, put the insert point anywhere in the system, and choose Compute → Solve → Exact. The variables are found automatically without having to be specified, as in the following example. Compute → Solve → Exact x + y − 2 ⁢ z = 1     2 ⁢ x − 4 ⁢ y + z = 0     2 ⁢ y − 3 ⁢ z = − 1 , Solution: [ x = 17 8 , y = 11 8 , z = 5 4 ] To solve a system of equations with two equations and three unknowns, you must specify Variables to Solve for in a dialog box. Put the insert point anywhere in the matrix and choose Compute → Solve → Exact. A dialog box opens asking you to specify the variables. Type the variable names, separated by commas.
