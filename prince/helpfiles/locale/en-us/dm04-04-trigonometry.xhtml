Hyperbolic Functions and Inverse Hyperbolic Functions

Certain functions, known as the hyperbolic sine, hyperbolic cosine, hyperbolic tangent, hyperbolic cotangent, hyperbolic secant, and hyperbolic cosecant, occur as combinations of the exponential functions e x and e − x having the same relationship to the hyperbola that the trigonometric functions have to the circle. It is for this reason that they are called hyperbolic functions.
Note
The hyperbolic functions are "trigtype" functions, allowing you to enter arguments without parentheses.

Hyperbolic Functions The function names used for the basic hyperbolic functions are sinh , cosh , tanh , coth , sech , and csch . Most of these function names automatically gray when typed in mathematics mode. When they do not, choose Insert > Math Objects > Math Name, type the name in the Name box, and select Apply.
