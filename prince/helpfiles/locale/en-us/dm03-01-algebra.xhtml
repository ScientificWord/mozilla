Polynomials and Rational Expressions

The general form of a polynomial of degree n in the variable x is a n ⁢ x n + a n − 1 ⁢ x n − 1 + ⋯ + a 1 ⁢ x + a 0 where a 0 , a 1 , … , a n are constants and a n ≠ 0 .
In this section: Polynomials Summation Notation Sums, Differences, Products, and Quotients
Polynomials You can perform the usual arithmetic operations on polynomials. The general procedure is as described below. To work with a polynomial expression Type the expression in mathematics mode and leave the insert point in the expression. Apply one of the commands from the Compute menu. Choose Insert > Math Objects > Subscript and type k = 0 . Press tab and type 5 . Press the spacebar and type a k ⁢ x k .
