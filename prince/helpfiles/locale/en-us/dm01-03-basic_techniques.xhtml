Basic Guidelines for Computing

When you respond to the request, "place the insert point in the expression," place the insert point within, or immediately to the right of, the expression. The position immediately to the left of a mathematical expression is not part of the mathematics.

Evaluating Expressions In Scientific WorkPlace and Scientific Notebook, you can perform computations with the mathematics you enter. The following sequence of actions inserts = 11 to the right of 3 + 8 , resulting in the equation 3 + 8 = 11 . To compute the sum 3 + 8 Choose Insert > Math Type 3 + 8 Choose Compute > Evaluate The mathematics shown in the gray area depicts both what you enter and the result after choosing the computing command Evaluate. Compute > Evaluate 3 + 8 = 11 By following the same procedure, you can add, subtract, multiply, and divide, and perform a vast variety of other mathematical computations. For a complete list of menu commands, see Compute Menu. Except that it be mathematically correct, there are almost no rules about the form for entering a mathematical expression.

Here are some examples illustrating a variety of ways to enter arithmetic expressions. Note that if the input is rational numbers, the output is a rational number; if the input is in decimal notation, the output is in decimal notation. However, the way it is interpreted may or may not be what you intended. To be safe, remove an ambiguity by placing additional parentheses in the expression. To check the interpretation of a mathematical expression Leave the insert point in the expression. Choose Compute > Interpret.
