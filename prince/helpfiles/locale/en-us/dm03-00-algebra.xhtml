Algebra

Algebraic operations are generalizations of arithmetic operations. Algebraic expressions are obtained by starting with variables and constants and combining them using addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, exponentiation, and roots. The simplest types of algebraic expressions use only addition, subtraction, and multiplication; these are called polynomials.
