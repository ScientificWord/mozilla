Units and Measurements

The available units include units from the System of International Units (SI units), an internationally agreed upon system of coherent units that is now in use for all scientific and most technological purposes in many countries. SI units are of three kinds: the base, supplementary, and derived units. There are seven base units for the seven dimensionally independent physical quantities: length, mass, time, electric current, thermodynamic temperature, amount of substance, and luminous intensity. Units from some other commonly used systems are also implemented. You can define other units in terms of the ones available in the Unit Name list. See Units for a list of units, unit prefixes, and keyboard shortcuts.

Entering Units Units appear on your screen as dark green characters (unless you have changed this default to another color). Units are in mathematics mode and are active mathematical objects. Compute → Solve → Exact 1 ⁢ mi = x ⁢ km , Solution: 1. 609 3 10 ⁢ cm = x ⁢ in , Solution: 3. 937 To enter a unit name Place the insert point at the position where you want the unit name. Choose Insert → Math objects → Unit name. Select a category from the Physical Quantity list. Select a name from the Unit Name list and choose OK. The unit name will appear at the position of the insert point. The Unit Name dialog box will remain on your screen for further use. To close it, click the × in the upper right corner of the dialog. To replace a unit Select the unit name you want to replace, either with click and drag or by placing the insert point to the right of the unit name. Choose Insert → Math objects → Unit name. Select a category from the Physical Quantity list. Select a name from the Unit Name list. Choose Replace. The new unit name will replace the previous unit name. The Unit Name dialog will remain on your screen for further use. To close it, click the × in the upper right corner of the dialog. Units are automatically recognized and can be entered from the keyboard. See Entering Units of Measure for keyboard shortcuts and other information about the units. [ Return to beginning of section ] Arithmetic Operations with Units You can carry out normal arithmetic operations with units using Evaluate. If the units differ, the results will be returned in terms of the basic unit in the category. Measurements will be returned in the metric system. Compound names are written as fractions or products, such as ft / s , ft · lbf , and acre · ft .
