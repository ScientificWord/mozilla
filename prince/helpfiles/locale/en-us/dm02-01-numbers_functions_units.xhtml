Addition and Subtraction To add 3 , 6 , and 14 Choose Insert > Math to put the insert point in mathematics mode. Type 3 + 6 + 14 (This expression should appear red in your document window.) Leave the insert point in the expression 3 + 6 + 14 . Choose Compute > Evaluate. This sequence prompts the system to insert = 23 to the right of the 3 + 6 + 14 , resulting in the equation 3 + 6 + 14 = 23 . By following the same steps, you can carry out subtraction and perform a vast variety of other mathematical computations. With the insert point in the sum (or difference), choose Compute > Evaluate. The template will appear with the insert point in the upper input box, ready for you to begin entering numbers or expressions. To multiply 3 , 6 , and 14 Choose Insert > Math to put the insert point in mathematics mode. Type 3 ⋅ 6 ⋅ 14 (This expression should appear red in your document window.) Leave the insert point in the expression 3 ⋅ 6 ⋅ 14 . Choose Compute > Evaluate. This sequence prompts the system to insert = 252 to the right of the 3 ⋅ 6 ⋅ 14 , resulting in the equation 3 ⋅ 6 ⋅ 14 = 252 . By following the same steps and appropriate notation, you can carry out division and perform a vast variety of other mathematical computations. Use any standard linear or fractional notation for multiplication and division, and with the insert point in the product or quotient, choose Compute > Evaluate. With the usual default, most commands applied to a mixed number return a fraction. For example, applying Evaluate or Simplify to 14 5 9 gives the result 131 9 . Compute > Evaluate 1 2 3 = 5 3 193 87 94 = 18229 94 1 2 3 + 2 3 4 = 53 12 The reverse is accomplished by Compute > Rewrite > Mixed, which converts a fraction to a mixed number. Compute > Rewrite > Mixed 5 3 = 1 2 3 18229 94 = 193 87 94 53 12 = 4 5 12 You can change the default so that fractions are output as mixed numbers. To set mixed numbers as default for rational numbers Choose Tools > Preferences > Computation, Output tab. Check "Output fractions as mixed numbers." With Mixed number checked as the output default for fractions, the system behaves as follows. Compute > Evaluate 5 3 5 + 1 2 3 = 7 4 15 Note The expansion of a fraction to a mixed number uses the familiar long-division algorithm.
